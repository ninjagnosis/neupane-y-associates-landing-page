## Ready for Audit? Check These Boxes

Audit readiness doesn't start in October. It starts the day after your previous year closes. Here's how to prepare so your auditors spend time on substance, not hunting for missing documentation.

### The Checklist

1. **Bank reconciliation**: All months reconciled. Unexplained differences investigated and resolved.
2. **Accounts payable aging**: Invoices matched. Payments recorded. Accruals made for unpaid bills.
3. **Accounts receivable aging**: Invoices traced to deposits. Bad debts identified and provided for.
4. **Inventory counts**: Physical counts performed and matched to ledger. Write-downs recorded.
5. **Fixed asset register**: All assets listed with acquisition cost, depreciation method, and accumulated depreciation.
6. **Loan agreements**: All outstanding loans documented with terms, interest rates, repayment schedules.
7. **Related-party transactions**: All related-party dealings identified and documented.
8. **Tax provisions**: Income tax, VAT, TDS — all liabilities calculated and accrued.
9. **Prior-year audit findings**: All adjustments from the prior year's audit incorporated into current books.
10. **Supporting documentation**: Invoices, receipts, contracts filed and organized by transaction type.
11. **Contingent liabilities**: Litigation, pending disputes, or warranty obligations noted in memo.
12. **Transaction testing**: Sample of transactions traced from invoice through to payment and ledger posting.

### Common Audit Findings (and How to Avoid Them)

**Unreconciled bank balances**: Bank reconciliations done weeks *after* month-end often reveal errors. Reconcile immediately.

**Missing invoices**: Invoices must exist for all significant transactions. Without an invoice, it's an adjustment.

**Unexplained journal entries**: Every journal entry needs a memo explaining business rationale. "Accrual for bonuses" beats "adjustment."

**Incomplete related-party disclosure**: Transactions with directors, relatives, or affiliate companies must be disclosed separately.

**Depreciation errors**: Assets depreciated over incorrect useful lives inflate or deflate net income.

### Audit Timeline

**Months 1-2 Post-Fiscal Year**: Bookkeeping cleanup and reconciliations.  
**Months 2-3**: Book close and trial balance preparation.  
**Month 3**: Auditor fieldwork begins.  
**Month 4**: Audit completion and financial statement sign-off.

Being ahead of this timeline means audit findings are rare and material issues are addressed proactively.
